About Me

I grew up farming and ranching in northern Utah, which fostered my love of plants and the production of food. I loved to farm, but I also always wanted to be a scientist. When I arrived at college, I fell in love with my agricultural classes, but I also had a deep interest in plant science. So, I did what any kid would do and combined my two passions. Upon arriving at UVU, I had the tremendous opportunity to join Dr. Yu-Ya Liang's lab. Becasue of this, I was able to start my very own, original research project from the ground up. This has been, and will continue to be, a huge asset to me in my journey to becoming a professional scientist.

Research

My area of research interest is an overlap of plant breeding and genetics, and plant pathology. My current research project, "Geminivirus Resistance in Mustard Plants" aims to identify genetic information of interest in regards to geminivirus resistance, and then use that information in the breeding of a disease resistant variety.
